FORMSPREE SETUP INSTRUCTIONS (MUCH EASIER!)
============================================

Formspree is a free service that collects form submissions and sends them to your email.
You can export all submissions to Excel/CSV.

STEP 1: Sign up for Formspree (FREE)
-------------------------------------
1. Go to https://formspree.io/
2. Click "Get Started" or "Sign Up"
3. Create a free account (you can use your searc.oist@gmail.com email)
4. Verify your email

STEP 2: Create a New Form
--------------------------
1. After logging in, click "New Project" (or you'll be prompted to create one)
2. Name your project: "Navi-ken Waiting List"
3. Click "New Form"
4. Name your form: "Waiting List Signup"
5. Copy your Form ID (it looks like: xyzabc12)

STEP 3: Update signup.html
---------------------------
1. Open signup.html
2. Find this line (around line 164):
   const response = await fetch('https://formspree.io/f/YOUR_FORM_ID', {
3. Replace YOUR_FORM_ID with your actual Form ID from Step 2
   Example: 'https://formspree.io/f/xyzabc12'

STEP 4: Test Your Form
-----------------------
1. Save signup.html
2. Open your website: http://localhost:8080
3. Click "Sign up for waiting list"
4. Fill out and submit the form
5. Check your email - you'll receive the submission!

VIEWING SUBMISSIONS
-------------------
1. Log in to https://formspree.io/
2. Go to your "Waiting List Signup" form
3. Click "Submissions" to see all entries
4. You can view each submission with all the details

EXPORTING TO EXCEL
------------------
1. In Formspree dashboard, go to your form
2. Click "Submissions"
3. Click "Export" button
4. Choose CSV format
5. Open the CSV file in Excel

ALTERNATIVE: Direct Email Setup (No Formspree needed)
------------------------------------------------------
If you prefer to receive emails directly without any service, you can also use:

1. Update the form action in signup.html to:
   <form id="signupForm" action="mailto:searc.oist@gmail.com" method="post" enctype="text/plain">

2. This will open the user's email client with the form data
   (Less user-friendly, but requires zero setup)
